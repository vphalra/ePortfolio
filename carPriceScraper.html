Web-scraping Car Prices

Scraping real-time car prices

Project Overview

This project automates the process of scraping car listings for Porsche 911 models from Cars.com, cleaning and analyzing the data to reveal pricing trends. The script collects critical data points, such as year, price, mileage, and customer reviews, to perform linear regression analysis on price depreciation over time.

Rationale

The project aims to streamline data collection for market research on car prices. Scraping data manually from platforms like Cars.com is inefficient and prone to human error. This project automates the process, providing reliable data for analysis. It also helps car buyers and sellers make informed decisions by evaluating factors like age and mileage on pricing trends.

The project has several key objectives:

  • Automate data collection: By automating the process, the project reduces manual labor and errors, while ensuring data is collected consistently.
  • Clean and transform data: Raw data from the website is standardized and structured for efficient and accurate analysis.
  • Analyze car depreciation trends: With data collected and cleaned, linear regression modeling is used to identify and predict price trends over time.

Result Interpretation

The analysis revealed key insights into the depreciation of Porsche 911 models:

  • The average yearly depreciation rate for Porsche 911 models is approximately $10,291.
  • Cars retain significant value during their early years before entering a phase of accelerated depreciation.
  • Factors such as mileage, maintenance condition, and storage practices influence price trends and resale value.

Older cars tend to have lower prices, but luxury cars like the Porsche 911 demonstrate stronger price resilience compared to non-luxury models.

Price vs. Mileage

There is a significant relationship between mileage and price, with higher mileage leading to lower prices. This trend reflects the impact of wear and tear on vehicle depreciation.

Conclusion

This project underscores the value of web scraping for collecting real-time data from websites. In dynamic industries like real estate, automotive, and e-commerce, where market conditions fluctuate rapidly, web scraping provides businesses with up-to-date insights on prices, availability, and trends. By automating data collection, it minimizes human error, enhances efficiency, and maintains data accuracy at scale.
